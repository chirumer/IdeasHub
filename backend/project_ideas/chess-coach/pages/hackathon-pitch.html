Hackathon Pitch

Chess Coach

The Problem

There are chess puzzles available online like the ones in lichess training. However, often these chess puzzles are unrealistic and are aimed towards building chess tactics.

To be good at chess puzzles does not translate to good chess gameplay as evidenced by discussions in chess community forums.

One can only improve their gameplay by practicing chess games and analyzing their faults and good moves.

Chess.com's coach mode helps users analyze their moves from past games with the best possible move. But the coaching is restricted to just the best move.

Although useful, it does not have the same appeal as chess puzzles, which are a gamified way of learning.

Our Solution

We propose an AI chess coach that coaches the hobbyist with chess puzzles inspired by their previous games.

Instead of generic tactical puzzles, users get personalized puzzles extracted from their own games. This bridges the gap between puzzle-solving skills and real gameplay improvement.

Key Features

  • Personalized Puzzles: Automatically generate chess puzzles from your actual games
  • Gamified Learning: Transform post-game analysis into engaging puzzle challenges
  • AI-Powered Feedback: Get detailed explanations of why your moves work or don't work
  • Real Game Context: Learn from positions you actually encountered, not abstract tactics
  • Progressive Difficulty: Adjust puzzle difficulty based on your skill level
  • Interactive Coaching: Ask questions about positions and get AI explanations

How It Works

  1. Connect your lichess account (or import games from other platforms)
  2. Our system analyzes your games to identify puzzle-worthy positions
  3. Puzzles are generated using chess engine analysis
  4. Solve puzzles and receive AI-generated feedback on your moves
  5. Learn from your mistakes in a gamified, engaging format

Implementation Form

We can make the implementation in the form of a gamified post-game analysis chrome extension similar to my "post video quizzer" hackathon idea that won NMIT hackathon (that generated gamified quiz to reinforce concepts explained in a Youtube video, this helps reinforce chess game mistakes in a gamified manner).

Hackathon Tip

We don't mention chess.com's coach mode in the pitch (some hackathon judges will say it's too similar), but if asked about it we say how we are different and defend our idea.
